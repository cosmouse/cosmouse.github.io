what happens in the brain in psychiatric illness?

individuals afflicted with psychiatric illness are often unable to put their experiences into the appropriate context. these symptoms reveal themselves not only as debilitating hallucinations, but also as subtle changes in basic sensory processing. we use two-photon calcium imaging in behaving mice to study how visual contextual processing is affected by serotonin 5-HT2A receptors, which are implicated in schizophrenia symptoms. our goals are to understand how neocortical circuits process context and how we might resolve contextual deficits for individuals affected by psychiatric disease.
